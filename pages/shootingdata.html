Interactive: 50 years of mass shootings in America

Since 1966, at least people have been killed or wounded in mass shooting incidents in the U.S.

The data powering this interactive was compiled by Mother Jones and include public shootings in which four or more victims were killed. We added five other incidents that fit these criteria — including the UT Tower shooting — that weren't present in the Mother Jones data. Five were from a separate database maintained by Stanford University.

This page was updated July 11, 2016.

Click the previous/next buttons — or use your left and right arrow keys — to toggle between years. You can also click on the bar chart to jump to a specific year (hold down shift to select multiple years).
